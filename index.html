    I’m a Computer Science student passionate about building smart, scalable, and user-focused digital solutions. My goal is to bridge the gap between technology and real-world needs, creating systems that not only function flawlessly but also deliver measurable value—whether by streamlining workflows, enabling data-driven decisions, or enhancing user satisfaction. I believe that technology is most impactful when it is accessible, efficient, and designed with purpose.

    During my Full Stack Developer internship at Polaris IT Company, I was involved in the complete lifecycle of web application development—from initial concept to final deployment. I designed intuitive and responsive front-end interfaces, integrated robust back-end APIs, optimized database queries for performance, and implemented secure authentication systems. This experience allowed me to collaborate closely with cross-functional teams, translate client needs into technical solutions, and deliver high-quality, scalable applications within tight deadlines.

    One of my most meaningful academic achievements was co-authoring a peer-reviewed research paper published in the International Journal of Nursing Practice titled “Relationship between Anxiety and Attitude towards Generative Artificial Intelligence among Nurses.” In this study, I played a key role in survey design, data collection, and statistical analysis. Our research explored how healthcare professionals perceive and adapt to AI technologies, identifying ethical considerations and factors influencing technology adoption in clinical settings.

I actively seek opportunities to experiment with new frameworks, contribute to innovative projects, and push the boundaries of what’s possible through creative problem-solving. For me, every project is more than just code—it’s a chance to create positive, lasting impact.
